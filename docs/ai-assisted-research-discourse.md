# AI-Assisted Research Discourse

## Working thesis

AI-assisted research will become normal.

The fight will be about the conditions: disclosure, source integrity, confidentiality, human responsibility and the right to inspect the work afterwards.

All links in this document were checked on 2026-06-30. See the [discourse source log](discourse-source-log.md).

## What is changing

Researchers, analysts and writers already use AI for search, summarization, drafting and synthesis. Publishers increasingly allow some of that work under rules.

The pattern is visible:

- AI can assist.
- Humans remain accountable.
- Sources still need checking.
- Confidential material needs special handling.
- Disclosure needs detail, not a generic sentence.

That creates the opening for this repository. It asks how AI-assisted research stays inspectable after the model has helped.

## Main critique lines

### 1. Accountability

AI systems cannot carry author responsibility. Humans remain responsible for claims, citations, interpretation and final decisions.

References:

- ICMJE, "AI Use by Authors" (accessed 2026-06-30): https://www.icmje.org/recommendations/browse/artificial-intelligence/ai-use-by-authors.html
- Elsevier generative AI policies (accessed 2026-06-30): https://www.elsevier.com/about/policies-and-standards/generative-ai-policies-for-journals

### 2. Source integrity

AI tools can produce plausible sentences with weak source contact: false claims, fabricated citations, distorted summaries or overconfident synthesis.

The larger risk is traceability loss. A clean sentence survives, but the path back to the source has disappeared.

References:

- Nature on invalid references in AI-assisted manuscripts (accessed 2026-06-30): https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-026-00969-z
- Nature survey coverage on researcher use and concerns (accessed 2026-06-30): https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-025-00343-5

### 3. Disclosure quality

A generic disclosure such as "AI was used" says too little. Useful disclosure names the task, the tool, the affected content and the human check.

Reference:

- CDC suggested disclosure elements for generative AI use in scientific work (accessed 2026-06-30): https://www.cdc.gov/ai/resources/considerations-for-generative-ai-use-in-scientific-work.html

### 4. Confidentiality and peer review

Review manuscripts, unpublished findings and confidential data change the risk profile. A public AI tool may be the wrong place for material that has not been cleared for that use.

References:

- Nature on AI and peer review concerns (accessed 2026-06-30): https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-025-00894-7
- IEEE RAS generative AI usage guidelines (accessed 2026-06-30): https://www.ieee-ras.org/publications/guidelines-for-generative-ai-usage/

### 5. Detector-based governance is weak

AI writing detectors do not solve research integrity. They can be biased, easy to route around, and disconnected from the question that matters: is the work source-grounded and accountable?

Reference:

- Stanford HAI on AI detector bias (accessed 2026-06-30): https://hai.stanford.edu/news/ai-detectors-biased-against-non-native-english-writers

## Outlook

Research agents and AI research assistants will get better. Systems such as OpenAI Deep Research, Claude Research, NotebookLM and related tools already point toward longer, source-heavy AI workflows.

Process discipline has to carry that weight.

When the tool can produce more, the audit trail has to carry more.

References:

- OpenAI Deep Research deployment-safety page (accessed 2026-06-30): https://deploymentsafety.openai.com/deep-research
- Anthropic multi-agent research system (accessed 2026-06-30): https://www.anthropic.com/engineering/multi-agent-research-system
- Google NotebookLM research features (accessed 2026-06-30): https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/products/notebooklm/better-research-notebooklm/

## Positioning implication

This project should not compete with research agents on search, scale or model capability.

Its role is narrower: claims stay visible, sources stay attached, uncertainty remains explicit, review findings remain auditable, and human decisions are documented.

That position should survive model changes because it aligns with research-integrity norms rather than one tool's feature set.
