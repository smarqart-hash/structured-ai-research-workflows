# Public / Private Boundary

## Purpose

This repository should be useful without exposing unpublished work.

The public version may describe the workflow. It must not turn ongoing research, reviewer material or private project artifacts into evidence before they are cleared.

## Public-safe

The repository may include:

- General workflow descriptions.
- Model-agnostic process patterns.
- Abstract examples.
- Synthetic examples.
- Empty templates.
- Public policy and publisher references.
- General error classes, without unpublished factual findings.

## Internal-only until cleared

Do not publish:

- Unpublished manuscript findings.
- Reviewer reports or responses to reviewers.
- Raw research runs.
- Internal roadmaps.
- Source excerpts from unpublished work.
- Screenshots from private tools.
- Dataset samples that are not already public and cleared.
- Tool logs, prompts or outputs that reveal unpublished claims.
- Private project paths, IDs, keys, credentials or infrastructure details.

## Ongoing manuscript boundary

Ongoing peer-review work may inform the author's judgment. It should not become a public case study before publication or explicit clearance.

Allowed wording:

> This workflow is informed by practical work on AI-assisted research and publication-oriented drafting.

Not allowed:

> This specific unpublished manuscript proves the workflow.

## Case-study rule

Before adding a real case study, check all five conditions:

- The underlying work is public or cleared.
- The case does not reveal confidential review material.
- Public sources support all factual claims.
- The case does not imply acceptance, validation or endorsement that has not happened.
- The case distinguishes older workflows from the Paper-COS pattern.

## Red-team questions

Before publication, ask:

- Would this reveal a finding before the paper is public?
- Would a reviewer or editor treat this as confidential?
- Does this imply stronger validation than exists?
- Could this expose a source, dataset or method that is not cleared?
- Does this make an internal tool or process look more mature than it is?
