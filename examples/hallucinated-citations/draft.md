# Draft

KPMG withdrew a report on agentic AI after fabricated case studies were identified in it, including false descriptions of AI work involving UBS, Swiss Federal Railways and the UK's NHS. Deloitte Australia later agreed to refund part of a government contract after a report was found to contain apparent AI-generated errors, including fabricated references.

The pattern is not limited to consulting reports. In Mata v. Avianca, lawyers were sanctioned after submitting non-existent cases generated by ChatGPT.

These examples do not prove how common hallucinated citations are. They show something narrower and still consequential: documented cases of AI-generated false references now include professional reports and legal filings. The practical problem is not only that a model can invent a citation. It is that polished work can hide where each claim came from, which claims were checked, and which claims were taken on trust.

## Disclosure

AI assisted the drafting of this example. The final wording was built from the claim ledger in this folder. One precise statistic was dropped because the cited source did not support it. Two broader claims were narrowed because the sources supported documented cases, not prevalence.
